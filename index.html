The MAKAVA Heritage

A house defined by patience.

From a single estate in Misiones to a quiet portfolio of legacy assets across Europe — MAKAVA has remained one thing throughout: deliberate.

Founded in 2008 by a family with three generations of ties to South American agriculture and Central European banking, MAKAVA was born from a singular conviction — that the finest things, whether a leaf or a legacy, are never produced at scale, but at depth.

Today the house cultivates a small, considered set of pursuits: single-origin yerba mate, generational real estate, and discreet private banking — each held to the same standard.

The Approach

A considered process.

We work slowly, deliberately, and only with those who share our appetite for the long view.

I

Introduction

A private conversation, in Vienna or by appointment. We listen first; we propose nothing.

II

Discernment

Six to twelve weeks of mutual study. We rarely accept new partnerships, and never quickly.

III

Stewardship

A bespoke mandate is drawn — for the leaf, the property, or the portfolio. Always written by hand.

IV

Continuity

A relationship intended to outlast its origin — passed across generations, undisturbed.
